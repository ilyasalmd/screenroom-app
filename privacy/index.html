Screenroom

Privacy Policy

Effective 12 June 2026

Screenroom collects no personal data, runs no analytics, and has no backend server. Your sources, library, and watch history never leave your devices.

What Screenroom is

Screenroom is a media player for iPhone, iPad, Mac, and Apple TV. It contains no channels, movies, or playlists. You connect sources you already have access to — an Xtream Codes subscription, an M3U playlist URL, or a direct stream address. The app plays them.

Data we collect

None. We have no servers, no accounts, and no telemetry. Specifically:

  • No registration, sign-in, or email address required.
  • No analytics or crash reporting SDKs are included.
  • No advertising identifiers are accessed or transmitted.
  • No usage data is sent to us or to any third party.

What stays on your device

Everything the app stores lives in your app container or iCloud account:

  • Source credentials (server address, username, password) — stored in the app sandbox on your device. They are sent only to your own source server to fetch your playlist.
  • Library cache — channel, movie, and series lists downloaded from your source. Stored locally in Caches and replaced on refresh.
  • Playback progress, favourites, and settings — stored in UserDefaults and synced across your own devices via iCloud Key-Value Storage (part of your personal iCloud account). No one else can read this data.
  • Downloads — offline copies of movies or episodes saved to the app container. Deleting the app deletes them permanently.
  • Artwork cache — cover images fetched from your source's own servers or from Apple's iTunes Search API (a public lookup service). No personal data is included in these requests.

iCloud sync

Sources, watch progress, My List, and settings sync across your own Apple devices using iCloud Key-Value Storage. This data is associated with your personal Apple ID and is governed by Apple's Privacy Policy. We cannot access it.

Third-party services

Screenroom can optionally fetch richer artwork and descriptions from the TMDB API if you enter a free API key in Settings. This is entirely optional. When enabled, movie and series title lookups are sent to TMDB's servers — no personal data is included. TMDB's privacy policy applies to those requests.

The app also uses Apple's iTunes Search API (keyless, public endpoint) for artwork fallback. Only the title of content being looked up is included in the request.

Streams and media

Media goes directly from your source's servers to the player on your device. Screenroom never proxies, caches, re-hosts, records, or redistributes streams.

Children

Screenroom is rated 12+ on the App Store. The app itself contains no content — it plays whatever is in the user-supplied source. Parents should be aware that IPTV sources may include channels or films unsuitable for children. Multiple profiles allow per-person watch history separation.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes materially, we will update the effective date above. Screenroom has no mechanism to push notifications about policy changes; check this page if you have questions.
